Flying DISChools will be a regular programme, which will use the sport of Ultimate Frisbee for development along with a series of modules designed to inculcate Life Skills Education for adolescents from underprivileged backgrounds.

The project will be implemented at locations such as with low income schools, government schools, slum communities and shelter homes with the aim of enabling a multi-pronged development opportunity for each underprivileged child.

Through weekly hour-long Frisbee sessions at each location, the aim would be to develop Life Skills with special focus on gender sensitivity, adolescent empowerment, responsible decision making, peer learning, self worth, self awareness and social inclusiveness.

Each session will progressively include Frisbee Drills/Scrimmage and match play followed by a reflective discussion with children, based on their own and their peers’ behavior on the play field. The points coming out from the introspection here will then be connected to real life instances, making this learning journey transformational.

The primary objective of this project is to impart crucial 21st century Life Skills to the children by harnessing the power of play. This would lead to the following goals:

  • Empowerment: To empower adolescents through play, we begin our intervention in their pre-adolescence phase. The aim is to enable them to make informed decisions aligned to their personal value system, while making the best use out of the available resources and their intellectual capacities.
  • Gender Sensitivity: We believe that women empowerment cannot happen in isolation and boys need to be included in the narrative so that they respect a girl because every human being should be respected, irrespective of their caste, color, sex or gender. Positive interaction on the playground between the genders can go a long way in building a strong sense of harmony.
  • Inclusiveness: We create equal and safe spaces where children can be who they are, irrespective of their backgrounds, abilities or socio-economic statuses. This helps them look at each other beyond divisive lines and connect at a human level, thereby promoting active pluralism.
