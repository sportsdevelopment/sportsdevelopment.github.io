The WFDF Development Programs

Welcome! Please read this page carefully before submitting the form to ask for support - we cannot help projects that do not qualify, so it is important to avoid wasting everybody’s time!

The WFDF has two programs to support development projects:

  1. The WFDF Development Grants Program supports a broad range of projects, including the introduction of Flying Disc sports into new countries, the expansion of Flying Disc sports activities in developing countries, gender inclusion projects, programs for spirit of the game education, youth programs, coaches training, anti-doping education and other areas. Grants can be for up to US$1,500, including the value of discs that may be a part of the request, and may go to fund activities for up to 15 months from the date of the award. The program is not limited to projects sponsored by WFDF member countries but explicitly calls for initiatives or projects by individuals or groups, particularly in new countries. Please take a look at the list of projects for some inspiration and reference. Applicants must complete the WFDF Development Grants Program Application Form and submit it via the Ask form. The main allocation criteria will be the project's sustainability and long lasting impact in the affected area. Grant recipients are required to submit reports on their activities and their outcomes.

  1. The WFDF Ambassadors Program is aimed at bringing donated equipment to individuals and communities that need this support to foster the growth of Flying Disc sports. This program provides only discs and other donated equipment, not monetary support. Sign up as Disc Ambassador if you can:
  • bring equipment with you in an upcoming trip
  • collect and store equipment for when it is needed

For example, there is a project in South Sudan that needs discs. Katie has seen this request, and as she is travelling to South Sudan soon, she is willing to bring some discs in her luggage. We look in our database, and find that close to where Katie lives we have Paula, who has collected some donated discs at a tournament. By connecting them, we can deliver the needed equipment at a minimal cost.

We have a huge and generous community. You can share the joy of playing disc sports, and the values that are at the core of our community by joining this program!

Priority is given to individuals with a proven contact with local groups who will make good use of the donated equipment, or who have a clear plan to start a disc sport group in a new area. The program recipients are required to submit a final report on how the equipment has been used with photos/videos, etc. Applicants must complete the WFDF Ambassadors Program application form and submit it via the Ask form.
